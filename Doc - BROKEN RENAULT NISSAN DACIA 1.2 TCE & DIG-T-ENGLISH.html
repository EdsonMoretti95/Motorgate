Understanding the failures ...

Technical Note No. 65012-2016-26736 - QMH001845​ ...

02/15/2016 Renault knows with certainty that the H5F engines have a major design flaw!

This confidential internal Renault memo  clearly admits this and even describes the entire process of engine degradation leading to the melting of an exhaust valve.

" Taking into account these elements, we were able to establish that the
degradation process was as follows:
Release of the scale from the tulips of the intake valves →
passage of the scale between the seats and
exhaust valves→ degradation of the exhaust valve surfaces
→ loss of sealing → passage of a heat flow between seat and
valve → melting of the exhaust valve "

It should be noted that in the context of these failures, no customer complaint for oil consumption had been recorded. ..so even without warning symptoms, the outcome remains almost inevitable.

The quality of the valves is not called into question, as is the segmentation...

The hypothesis retained is oil consumption by re-aspiration (by the oil vapor recycling circuit, as we will learn later).
So very clearly in 2016 Renault knew the risk of valve fusion , and yet no recall, no alert or warning was EVER sent to customers...
​Renault
therefore made the choice to preserve its interests to the detriment of the most elementary precautionary principles which should have led them to remove these DANGEROUS vehicles  from our roads (a valve meltdown at full speed on the motorway is a vehicle which suddenly goes from 130 km/h to 50!!!), who can claim that this does not directly endanger road users?!? Hundreds
of testimonies today confirm the risk that Renault put its customers under... To date, the courts have been contacted (criminal complaint filed on June 5, 2023 at the Nanterre Judicial Court), the DGCCRF alerted to repeatedly, the ministries of economy and finance as well as that of ecological and inclusive transition have also been alerted...and NO reaction!

This  confidential internal Renault note  (here in French, but which also exists in Dutch, German, Romanian and Serbian, plus an intervention report from Romania proving its application) was created on  06/05/2015 .

A “ failure of the injection calibration ”  is clearly admitted.
This note has evolved over the years, the number of engine codes and therefore vehicles concerned accumulating inexorably...until  ALL  H5Ft engines are part of this list!
The note initially invited   network members in black and white to explain to the customer " that a slightly higher oil consumption is normal and that a solution will be available to remedy the disturbance"...

Until the version dated on February 3, 2017 , it is this "minimization" concerning oil consumption which officially had to be explained to customers complaining of engine noise and/or excessive oil consumption but whose engine was not -yet- listed in the models concerned.
The "solution" ultimately developed is a reprogramming of the ECU (Engine Computing Unit) intended to alleviate the oil consumption problem. This reprogramming unfortunately seems very ineffective because it is generally applied too late, after discovery of the symptoms and therefore already pronounced engine deterioration. This reprogramming also has the effect in many cases of causing a dilution of gasoline in the oil...
In March 2017, the Actis Solution 35426 note appeared within the manufacturer network.

This time, ALL vehicles equipped with this engine are affected by an “injection calibration failure”.

2012-2016 models like 2016-2018 vehicles!
Renault had however communicated, in response to certain questions from MPs or through the Ministry of the Economy, that a new engine calibration had been applied from June 2015 in order to "limit oil consumption"

... The only curative solution therefore remains to reprogram the injection computer... a solution which does not work , as confirmed by the court decision handed down at the Nanterre Court on January 27, 2022 (RG number: 19/08172).

The manufacturer has therefore decided not to tell its customers the whole truth  because these abnormal oil consumption and engine noises, as we know, are symptoms of premature engine wear and are anything but normal!

For many victims, the manufacturer therefore showed a cruel lack of kindness, because many were refused support even though they had very early reported anomalies and these had then been described as "normal". "in an inappropriate, even misleading way!

Nissan TB EM 15/05  and NDQ 1833 ...

Here is the PDF version of the Nissan Technical Bulletin TB EM 15/05 dated 12/18/2015 and updated on 12/23/2019.
A free extension of the guarantee is also mentioned there, note NDQ 1833 .

These notes confirm that ALL 1.2 H5ft (HRA2DDT) engines can be affected by design defects leading to excessive oil consumption, ultimately leading to premature engine replacement.
ALL 1.2 DIG-T engines are affected (Qashqai J11, Pulsar F13 and juke F15), Euro 5 and Euro 6 engines!

Support can extend up to 60 months / 90,000 miles (whichever comes first) The Nissan 1.2 DIG-T

engines  (HRA2DDT engines) are the same engines as the Renault 1.2 T Ce (H5FT).  More information on this engine here .
The organization charts which accompany these notes obviously prove the perfect knowledge of the problem by the manufacturer from 2015 and what solutions to apply to the problems encountered...

A second document PCB-NES-16-050 (Product Campaign Bulletin) coming from Nissan Ireland and dating of 2016 details the process of reprogramming the ECU (Engine Computing Unit) in the event of abnormal oil consumption.
The engine degradation process is explained very precisely...see translation on page 2.
